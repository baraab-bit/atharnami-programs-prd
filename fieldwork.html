Fieldwork — the Chase Board & the 5-Stage Pipeline.

A per-assessment surface where the Assessment is the card that advances itself.

Fieldwork today is a blended, guessed-wave surface — six pages, a bare program dropdown, a "current wave" that is invisible and inferred, and a queue that flattens every beneficiary of every program into one undifferentiated list. This PRD turns it into two things: a per-assessment chase board that answers three questions at a glance — where's my rate vs. target & deadline, who still owes me, what's my one next move — and a 5-stage lifecycle pipeline (التأطير → التصميم → العمل الميداني → التحليل → التقرير) that retires the ClickUp خط الإنتاج.

§2 — From a real program

Three walls, opening one program.

Abdu opens Fieldwork to run collection on الاستدامة المالية (Financial Sustainability) under bin Mahfouz — a Post-only effort against ~4 funded organizations, each assessed by interview, each with a researched baseline. He hits three walls before doing anything useful.

Wall 1 — Scope

Which assessment is this?

He can't tell which assessment a number belongs to. Every assigned beneficiary of every program flattens into one queue — no assessment boundary at all.

Wall 2 — Who owes

Who still owes a response?

The board is organized around the relay — who forwards the link — not around who still owes a response.

Wall 3 — Next move

What's my one next move?

Nothing ranks the work or points at the next action. The effort history that would justify a rate to a funder is a dead stub.

"I can't tell which assessment a number belongs to, who still owes me a response, or what my next move is."

§4 — The chase board

The heart: the Operator's per-assessment chase board.

The user is the Operator running collection (Abdu / Baraa) — not the analyst, not the org. For one assessment at a time the board answers three questions at a glance.

For one assessment at a time: (1) which assessment am I in, and where's my rate vs. target & deadline? (2) who still owes me a response? (3) what's my one next move?  — Acceptance test: "does it let the Operator make the next move at a glance?"

4a · Triage landing → per-assessment board

The landing is a triage list of the Operator's in-flight assessments, sorted worst-first. Click a row → the single-assessment board. A persistent breadcrumb Program › Edition › Assessment + an assessment switcher sits on the board.

Deadline + instrument are Assessment attributes — fieldwork reads them, it does not own them. The instrument routes the board: fill-and-return (survey / records) → non-responder chase; interview → schedule-then-conduct.

4b · Two clocks & the three person-states

There are two clocks — the assessment's window (one deadline for everyone) and each person's slot (a booked time you can reschedule). A Subject's state is where their slot sits relative to the window. The per-person slot machinery already exists in code (interview-lifecycle.ts).

Scheduled

Future slot, within the window.

At-risk

Slot has passed, or the next booked slot is beyond the deadline.

Breached

Deadline passed and they still owe.

The deadline is a soft flag — it never blocks booking or logging past it — but a breach rolls up RED to the board header ("118 owing · 12 past deadline") and pushes the assessment up the triage. A separate, milder stale-contact cadence (chase_after_days, renamed from overdue_after_days) orders the daily mid-window queue, independent of the hard deadline.

§5 — Auto-advance & approval gates

The card advances on evidence, holds on approval.

It flows forward the instant a stage's real artifact exists; a gate is a hold that blocks that flow until a Lead (or optionally the Client) signs off — and "sign off" literally means promoting the artifact into المخرجات المعتمدة (Approved Deliverables).

5a · Auto-advance — the artifact is the trigger

From → ToFires when the real artifact exists
Framing → DesignResults framework defined
Design → FieldworkTool / instrument published
Fieldwork → AnalysisCollection closed — target hit OR deadline passed (the same breach signal from §4b)
Analysis → ReportInsights generated

No manual dragging. Auto-advance is precisely what replaces the manual ClickUp drag.

5b · Approval gates — holds that block auto-advance

"Approve" = promote the artifact to المخرجات المعتمدة (Approved Deliverables) — that folder is the definition of "done for this stage."

The role split — the Lead approves, the Operator runs

Assessment Lead — the owner

Approves / signs off

Owns the assessment in Atharnami. Holds the gates, promotes artifacts into المخرجات المعتمدة. Approves — but does not run collection.

Operator — Abdu

Publishes, sends, chases

Runs collection: publishes the tool, blasts it, works the chase board, exports Chase Rounds. Runs — but does not approve.

The approver is not the runner. This split drives the whole chase board.

§7 — Subject & the unified tool model

A list of people — sometimes a doorway to an org.

The chase list is always a list of people — but sometimes the person is a doorway to an organization, and it's the organization's numbers you're recording.

CaseUnit of analysisData grainExample
Plain SubjectThe personPersonFuture Skills — a student answers about themselves.
Subject represents an OrganizationThe OrganizationOrg grainbin Mahfouz الاستدامة المالية — one contact per funded org; you record the org's budget / sustainability data, not the person's.
The Roster always has a person to contact (that's why Subject is always a person). The "represents an org" flag flips the unit of analysis to the organization while keeping the contact human — this is how bin Mahfouz's 4 funded orgs are chased.

The unified tool model

Survey, interview, and records are all just tools — you send something out and collect something back. The only difference the board cares about is fill-and-return vs. scheduled conversation. New: records = a survey-shaped tool sent to the org to collect factual data (e.g. its budget); the org fills it in.

Tool typeBoard routing
survey + recordsFill-and-return → non-responder chase + Chase Rounds + OurSMS export.
interviewInterview lifecycle → schedule → conduct → complete; feeds the interview → indicator Link.
§8 — Roster onboarding

How the list becomes a chase.

The base PRD says the Roster is "imported" but never says where. Here is where: onto the Assessment itself. The empty assessment shows "No Roster yet — import the list"; import fills it; the denominator exists; the chase board lights up.

The list the org sends plugs in nowhere global — it lands straight onto one Assessment as its Roster. Scope is fixed to Program › Edition › Assessment from the first second, so a list can never become an orphan.

Parent Contact — الجهة الأم

Sometimes the org's list says "here are the 50 people — to reach them, go through this body that ran the program for them." That body is one reference column on the Roster, carrying a name (+ optional phone). It is a field on the Subject, not a modeled entity: no management page, no follow-up, no RAG, no lifecycle. Import fills it; often all rows share one value.

Documented honestly: الجهة الأم is a deliberate lightweight trace of the removed Partner — a reference column, not a re-introduction. "Organization" is already taken twice (the client + org-as-unit-of-analysis) and "Partner" would signal the deleted feature had returned, so "Parent Contact" is the deliberately-neutral choice. It is the seed for a possible future group-by / chase-through feature, but stays a plain column for now.

Chase board first, pipeline second, connectors later.

§12 — Success criterion

The path that does not exist today.

One Operator, one assessment, end to end — Abdu chasing bin Mahfouz's 4 funded orgs.

1
Abdu opens Fieldwork → the triage landing shows bin Mahfouz › الاستدامة المالية › Post near the top — 4 owing, deadline near, red dot.
2
He clicks in → the board runs in interview mode, showing 4 org-Subjects (each a person representing a funded organization) → he books slots.
3
One slot slips past the window → that Subject flips At-risk → Breached, the header goes red ("4 owing · 1 past deadline"), and the card climbs the triage.
4

That path does not exist today. This PRD is the path.
§13 — Open questions / gaps flagged

Six gaps — not resolved here.

Genuine gaps or tensions noticed while writing the ledger, not new decisions. Surfaced for a call.

◆ For Anas to weigh in
"Collection-opened" vs. the Chase Round clock. Before the first export there's no batch-exported date — so the first nudge presumably keys off collection-opened + chase_after_days, then later rounds off the last export. Implied but never stated. Confirm.
Records tool with no "person" to chase. The Roster always has a person, and records is sent to the org. For bin Mahfouz the contact exists — but is there ever a records case pulled from a system / document with no human respondent? If so, the chase board has no one to chase. Confirm records always has a human Subject.
Auto-advance vs. approval gate ordering at Fieldwork-entry. Design → Fieldwork auto-advances when the tool is published, but Design-exit has an instrument sign-off gate. Does publishing imply the sign-off, or can a tool be published while Design-exit is still unapproved? The two triggers could race. Clarify whether "publish" is gated behind Design-exit approval.
Interview-mode "Non-respondent" semantics. "# still owe" and the RED breach rollup are crisp for fill-and-return. For interviews, a Subject can be scheduled (not yet a respondent, but not idle). Does "# still owe" count SCHEDULED subjects, or only those with no slot? The RAG math for interviews needs an explicit rule.
Group (الفئة) vs. Assessment identity. The base PRD treats group as part of the Assessment's identity; §6 here calls it the "third axis inside an Assessment." These are consistent — §6 carries a reconciliation note — but confirm Anas reads them as one thing, not a contradiction.
Which component owns the deadline-breach signal. §5a (auto-advance) and §4b (chase board) consume the same breach signal ("target hit OR deadline passed"). Stated as one signal — good — but no single component is named as its emitter. Name where the breach is computed once and read by both.

The Assessment is the card that moves. This PRD is the path.
